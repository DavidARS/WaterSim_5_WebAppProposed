The Personal Water Use Sustainability Indicator

Personal Water Use Sustainability Indicator measures the level of personal water use in gallons per capita per day (GPCD). Decreasing the household water that you use on a personal (per capita) basis decreases the water demand for your community. Decreasing demand increases water sustainability and the water available for future use, by you or those that move into your community in the future. The minimum, attainable, (total) GPCD is 30.


Table of GPCD Goals:

GPCD Goal Measures to Improve Efficiency (reduce water use)
118 Continue existing trends in indoor water use (residential and commerical); use less water outdoors (fewer fountains, less turf (grass), etc.)
90 Greatly reduce potable water irrigation (limit turf, use rainwater harvesting, green infrastructures, drought tolerant plants), reduce commercial and industrial water use.
70 Elimiate most oudoor water use (no grass, no misters, fewer trees, remove pools), significantly alter indoor water use behaviors (reduced shower length, clothes washing, toilet flushing)
50 Greatly reduce non-residential water use (less car washing, more efficient industry, switch from water cooled to air cooled systems, accept brown golf courses and parks)
30 Water budgets for residential water use (water use above a per capita limit regulated), only essential non-residential water use (hospitals, food production, no potable washing, no grass parks)
